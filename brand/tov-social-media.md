## 1. Overview & Social Media Philosophy

### 1.1 The Governing Principle

> Social media is not a billboard. It's a conversation at a busy dinner table — you earn attention by being the most interesting, most useful person in the room, not the loudest.

NextMaven AI's social media voice is built on one unbreakable rule:

> *"Every post must pass the Save Test: After reading this, does the audience (1) learn something, (2) save time, (3) get inspired to try, or (4) join the conversation? If not — rewrite."*

### 1.2 Social Media ≠ Marketing Copy

The voice in `tov-marketing-general.md` provides the foundation. But social media has its own physics:

| Marketing General | Social Media |
|---|---|
| Reader came to you (landing page, ad click) | You must earn attention in a scroll |
| Longer attention window | 3 seconds to hook or lose them |
| Conversion is the direct goal | Value delivery is the primary goal; conversion follows trust |
| Structured, hierarchical layout | Flat, fast, skimmable |
| Brand speaks | A person behind the brand speaks |
| Professional-first | Platform-native first — each platform has its own culture |

### 1.3 The Three Social Media Commitments

Every NextMaven AI social post must honour these commitments:

| Commitment | What It Means |
|---|---|
| Value-first, always | No post exists purely to sell. Every post teaches, provokes, or equips. |
| Platform-native execution | What works on LinkedIn fails on Threads. We adapt voice, structure, length, and format per platform — no cross-posting identical content. |
| Research-backed relevance | No post is generated from assumptions. Every post reflects real conversations, real pain points, and real trends happening right now in our audience's world. |

## 3. Content Type System

### 3.1 The Six Content Types

Every NextMaven AI social post must be classified into one of six types before writing begins. Each type has its own structural rules and tone emphasis:

| # | Content Type | Purpose | Tone Emphasis |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Tutorial / Guideline | Teach a specific skill or approach | Coach tone · clarity ↑↑ · empowerment ↑ |
| 2 | Workflow Breakdown | Show a systemized AI process | Authority ↑ · specificity ↑↑ · "SOP-style" |
| 3 | Tools Comparison | Compare AI tools objectively | Honesty ↑↑ · practical ↑ · no hype |
| 4 | Tools / Features Spotlight | Highlight a specific tool or feature | Surprise ↑ · relevance ↑ · "why now" |
| 5 | Engagement / Community | Start a conversation, invite participation | Playfulness ↑↑ · warmth ↑↑ · question-driven |
| 6 | AI News / Opinion | React to AI developments with a POV | Speed ↑ · sharpness ↑ · provocative question |

### 3.2 Content Type Writing Rules

#### Type 1: Tutorial / Guideline

- Lead with the common mistake or gap (not the solution)
- Use Before → After where possible
- Actionable steps — numbered, skimmable
- Coach tone — alongside the reader, not above
- Structure for save-worthiness — frameworks, prompts, templates
- Clear enough that someone could execute after reading

#### Type 2: Workflow Breakdown

- Always show old way vs. AI way contrast
- Use visual sequencing (Step 1 → Step 2 → Step 3)
- Focus on measurable outcomes: time saved, cost reduced, output increased
- Make it look systemized — SOP-style clarity
- Include specific tool names (ChatGPT, Make.com, n8n, etc.)

#### Type 3: Tools Comparison

- Same test → different tools (fair comparison)
- Clear verdict — don't sit on the fence
- Specify who should use which tool and why
- Honest pros/cons — never hide downsides
- No hype — real talk only. If a tool is weak, say so.
- HK-relevant: note pricing (HKD), accessibility, free tier, no VPN needed

#### Type 4: Tools / Features Spotlight

- Focus on surprising or underrated use cases — not official feature lists
- Show the transformation the feature enables
- Emphasize "why this matters now" — timeliness
- Personal experience: "I tested this and here's what happened"

#### Type 5: Engagement / Community Post

- Encourage replies — ask specific, opinionated questions
- Give prompt templates or conversation starters
- Share a visual or emotional moment from the AI journey
- Playful or thought-provoking — never generic ("What do you think?")
- Frame questions as choices or dilemmas, not open-ended voids

#### Type 6: AI News / Opinion

- Speed matters — post within hours of news breaking
- 1-sentence summary of what happened
- Add a strong personal POV — don't just report
- End with a provocative or polarizing question
- Keep it short and sharp — no essays

### 4.2 ⭐ THREADS — The Core Platform

Platform identity: Threads is NextMaven AI's most natural-sounding channel. It's where we sound most like a person and least like a brand.

#### Threads Tone Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Voice | Conversational — like talking to a friend or colleague in the same industry |
| Register | Casual but not childish. Natural, honest. Not selling. |
| Language | Cantonese + Traditional Chinese. No code-mixing except proper nouns. |
| Feel | Like a HK creator explaining something they discovered to friends |
| Energy | Curious, observational, genuine — never performative |

The Threads tone must be:
- Conversational（像同朋友 / 同事 / 社群講嘢）
- Natural and honest — no pitch tone
- Allows richer detail, delivered as natural sentences (not lists or headers)
- Light Cantonese colour — but never so heavy that non-native readers get lost

#### Threads Tone Guardrails

✅ Correct Threads vibe:

| Example | Why It Works |
|---|---|
| 「呢排大家都講緊 XX，我試咗下…」 | Natural opening — like starting a conversation |
| 「有啲位幾出乎意料…」 | Genuine observation — not a claim |
| 「我自己覺得最實用嘅係呢度。」 | Personal POV — honest, not authoritative |
| 「你哋覺得呢？」 | Invites reply — not demands engagement |

❌ Forbidden Threads tone:

| Forbidden | Why It Fails |
|---|---|
| 「震撼」「革命」「飛躍」「最強」「立即收藏」 | Marketing-speak. Kills authenticity instantly. |
| Sales tone / Teacher tone / Corporate tone | Threads users scroll past anything that feels "branded" |
| Small headings, structured sections, formatted lists | Not Threads-native. Threads is running text, not blog posts. |
| Overly polished or educational tone | Feels fake. Threads rewards realness. |
| LinkedIn-style structure / "Hook / TL;DR / Highlights / CTA" labels | Platform mismatch. This structure screams "I cross-posted." |

#### Threads Structure (Mandatory)

Every Threads post follows this flow:

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| 1. Hook | A natural opening sentence — like starting a chat, NOT a copywriting hook |
| 2. Body | 1–3 observations, impressions, or takeaways — written as conversation, NOT a list |
| 3. Close | A light question for interaction |

Hook rules for Threads:

Threads hooks must NOT sound like copywriting.

| ❌ Forbidden Hooks | ✅ Allowed Hooks |
|---|---|
| 「唔係升級，而係飛躍」 | 「Gemini 3 出咗，你哋試咗未？」 |
| 「打破你對 AI 嘅想像」 | 「啱啱玩完 Gemini 3，有啲位幾驚喜。」 |
| 「3 大重點功能」 | 「呢排大家都討論 Gemini 3，我都試咗下。」 |
| 「史上最強」 | 「想同大家 share 下我試 Gemini 3 嘅感覺。」 |
| 「震撼登場」 | 「啱啱見到個新 workflow 幾有趣…」 |

#### Threads Detail Level (Critical)

To maximize value density without breaking the conversational tone:

| ✅ DO | ❌ DON'T |
|---|---|
| Include 3–5 mini insights — written as conversation | Write a product review or official summary |
| Mention technical things in 人話 sentence format | Use jargon or technical bullet points |
| Describe how the tool feels, what changed, and why it matters | List features or specs |
| Personal impressions and examples | Report tone or analysis tone |

Correct sentence patterns for Threads:

- 「試完覺得有幾個位幾實際…」
- 「最明顯係佢…」
- 「我唔預期佢… 結果幾驚喜。」
- 「對平時做 XX 嘅人應該幾有用。」
- 「成個 workflow 感覺順咗…」

#### Threads Interaction Close (Mandatory)

Every Threads post must end with one natural question:

| ✅ Natural Questions | ❌ Forced Questions |
|---|---|
| 「你哋試咗未？感覺如何？」 | 「你覺得呢？快啲留言！」 |
| 「你覺得邊個位最實用？」 | 「立即分享你嘅想法！」 |
| 「你會 upgrade 嗎？」 | 「Tag 一個朋友嚟睇！」 |
| 「有冇人玩咗？講下你哋嘅感覺。」 | 「唔好錯過！你點睇？」 |

#### Threads Formatting Rules

- Short paragraphs — 1–3 sentences max per block
- No headers, no bullet points, no numbered lists — running text only
- Line breaks between thought blocks for readability
- Hyper-skimmable — every sentence should stand alone in meaning
- High info density + human voice = the Threads formula
- Always Traditional Chinese + Cantonese writing
- Save-first content: frameworks, prompts, templates embedded in conversation

### 4.3 ⭐ LINKEDIN — The Authority Platform

Platform identity: LinkedIn is where NextMaven AI speaks with the most structure and professional credibility. English-language. Business-impact focused.

#### LinkedIn Tone Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Voice | Professional, clear, human — a senior practitioner sharing insights |
| Register | Business professional. Not stiff. Not casual. Think: keynote speaker in a conversation, not a memo. |
| Language | Full English. No Cantonese. |
| Feel | A respected peer sharing earned knowledge with decision-makers |
| Energy | Measured, confident, thought-leadership driven |

The LinkedIn tone must deliver:
- Business relevance — tie everything to business outcomes
- Decision-making clarity — help the reader make better choices
- Frameworks & models — structured thinking that positions NextMaven as an authority
- Examples with outcomes — not theory, but demonstrated results
- Mature, credible voice — no slang, no emoji spam, no hype

#### LinkedIn Tone Guardrails

| ✅ LinkedIn Voice | ❌ Not LinkedIn Voice |
|---|---|
| Strong opening stance / insight | Vague "In today's world..." opening |
| Data-backed or experience-backed claims | Unsubstantiated bold statements |
| Structured framework with clear logic | Random thoughts without throughline |
| Open-ended CTA that invites senior discussion | "Like and share if you agree!" |
| Practical takeaway a manager can use Monday | Fluffy motivational quotes |

#### LinkedIn Structure (Mandatory)

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| 1. Hook | 1–2 lines. Pain point, insight, or a strong stance. Must stop the scroll. |
| 2. POV / Experience | Your personal take or what you've seen working with clients/learners |
| 3. Framework / Insight | Structured practical takeaway — the "meat" |
| 4. Example / Mini-case | Concrete: scenario + outcome + tool/approach |
| 5. CTA | Open-ended question inviting professional discussion |

#### LinkedIn Formatting Rules

- First 2 lines are everything — LinkedIn truncates after ~2 lines. Hook must earn the "see more" click.
- One idea per line — use line breaks generously
- Short paragraphs (1–3 sentences)
- Bold is acceptable for emphasis but use sparingly
- Emojis: Maximum 2–3 per post. Use as visual markers (✅ ❌ → 📊), never as decoration
- Hashtags: 3–5 relevant hashtags at the bottom of the post
- Length: 150–250 words optimal. Can go to 400 for frameworks/deep dives.
- No LinkedIn "broetry" — one-word-per-line manipulation is banned

### 4.4 ⭐ INSTAGRAM — The Visual Platform

Platform identity: Instagram is NextMaven AI's visual teaching channel. Reels-first. Carousel for frameworks. Feed for brand moments.

#### Instagram Tone Rules

| Rule | Detail |
|---|---|
| Voice | High energy, fast value, visually-driven |
| Register | Slightly more casual than Threads — visual storytelling does the heavy lifting |
| Language | Cantonese (Traditional Chinese) for captions. English acceptable for Reel text overlays. |
| Feel | A HK creator who makes AI look exciting and accessible |
| Energy | Energetic, punchy, discovery-driven |

#### Instagram Content Priority

| Format | Priority | Use For |
|---|---|---|
| Reels | ⭐ Primary | Quick workflows, 1 prompt = transformation, AI experiments, news in motion |
| Carousel | Secondary | Step-by-step tutorials, frameworks, comparison breakdowns |
| Static / Single Image | Tertiary | Quotes, announcements, community highlights |
| Stories | Supporting | BTS, polls, quick tips, engagement |

#### Instagram Structure — Reels

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| 1. Hook (0–1 sec) | Pattern interrupt — "Stop doing ___" / "This AI trick saves 3 hours" |
| 2. Simple steps (2–15 sec) | Visual demonstration with text overlays |
| 3. Before → After (if applicable) | Show the transformation visually |
| 4. Takeaway (last 2–3 sec) | Prompt template, workflow name, or "save for later" |

#### Instagram Structure — Carousel

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Slide 1 | Hook — curiosity or pain point. Bold text. Scroll-stopping. |
| Slide 2 | Context — "here's what most people get wrong" |
| Slides 3–8 | Value delivery — 1 step/idea per slide |
| Final slide | CTA — "Save this" / "Follow for more" / "Link in bio" |

#### Instagram Caption Rules

- Short, punchy captions — 2–5 lines for Reels. Up to 10 lines for Carousel.
- First line is the hook — must work as standalone text
- One CTA per caption — save, share, comment, or link in bio
- Hashtags: 5–10 relevant hashtags. Mix broad (#AI #Automation) + niche (#AIforSME #HKbusiness)
- Emoji use: Light, purposeful. 2–5 per caption. Never decorative chains.

#### Instagram Formatting Rules

- Visual-first thinking — if the visual doesn't carry, the post fails regardless of caption
- Text overlay on Reels: ≤ 7 words per frame. High contrast. Readable at phone distance.
- Carousel text: Clean, minimal. One idea per slide. Brand fonts.
- Save + Share optimization — every post should contain at least one actionable takeaway

## 5. Social Media Content Rules — Cross-Platform

These rules apply across all platforms unless a platform-specific rule overrides them:

### 5.1 Research-First Requirement (Mandatory)

Before generating any social post, the content must be informed by real research:

| Step | Action |
|---|---|
| 1. Scan | Check recent content on Threads, Reddit, X, LinkedIn trending, Instagram Reels trends |
| 2. Identify | Current pain points, active conversations, common misconceptions, emerging trends |
| 3. Validate | Ensure the post reflects real-world conversations, not assumptions |
| 4. Adapt | Adjust writing style based on platform culture, algorithm preference, and content type |

Why this matters: Social media rewards relevance and timeliness. A perfectly written post about yesterday's topic gets zero engagement. Research ensures we're always in the conversation, not behind it.

### 5.2 The Value Density Rule

Every post must deliver maximum information per word. Social media has no room for filler.

| ✅ High Value Density | ❌ Low Value Density |
|---|---|
| Specific workflow with tool names and steps | "AI can help you work faster" |
| A prompt template the reader can copy | "Prompts are really important for AI" |
| Comparison with clear verdict | "There are many tools to choose from" |
| Personal result with numbers | "I've been experimenting with AI lately" |

### 5.3 No Selling Tone

This is the #1 social media tone guardrail.

Social posts can mention NextMaven AI, link to resources, and include CTAs — but the energy must always be value-first. The moment a post reads like an ad, engagement dies.

| ✅ Value-First Mention | ❌ Selling Tone |
|---|---|
| "We built a workflow for this — here's the free template" | "Our amazing AI OS has everything you need — sign up now!" |
| "This is from our content automation course — the full version has 12 more like it" | "Enrol today and transform your business with our comprehensive AI system!" |
| "Link in bio if you want the full workflow breakdown" | "Don't miss out! Limited spots available!" |

### 5.4 One Post, One Idea

Every social post communicates one core idea. Not three. Not "a few thoughts on..."

| ✅ Single-Idea Post | ❌ Multi-Idea Post |
|---|---|
| "Here's a 3-step workflow that cuts content creation from 3 hours to 20 minutes" | "AI is changing content creation, automation is important, and here are some tools you should try" |

### 5.5 Engagement is Designed, Not Requested

Never ask for engagement as a transaction ("Like if you agree!"). Design the content so engagement is the natural response.

| ✅ Designed Engagement | ❌ Requested Engagement |
|---|---|
| Sharing a polarizing opinion that invites debate | "Do you agree? Like and comment!" |
| Asking a specific dilemma: "When you get client feedback at 6pm Friday — do you respond or wait till Monday?" | "What do you think? Let me know below!" |
| Giving a prompt and asking "what results did you get?" | "Share this with a friend who needs it!" |

## 6. Social Media Tone — Forbidden Patterns

These patterns are banned across all NextMaven AI social platforms:

### 6.1 Forbidden Tones

| ❌ Tone | Why It's Banned |
|---|---|
| Sales pitch tone | Social media is for value delivery, not direct selling |
| Teacher/lecturer tone | We're peers sharing discoveries, not educators grading students |
| Overly polished / corporate | Feels manufactured. Social rewards authenticity. |
| Hype / clickbait | Erodes trust. If we hype, we're no different from every other AI marketer. |
| Report / analysis tone | Dry. No one opens social to read a report. |
| Motivational poster tone | "Believe in the power of AI to change your life!" — delete on sight |

### 6.2 Forbidden Opening Patterns

| ❌ Never Open With | Why |
|---|---|
| "In today's rapidly evolving AI landscape..." | Cliché, boring, says nothing |
| "AI is transforming the way we work..." | Been said 10 million times |
| "Did you know that 73% of businesses..." | Stat-first openings feel academic on social |
| "🚨 BREAKING: Major AI update..." | Red-siren clickbait energy |
| "Here are 5 things you need to know about..." | Generic listicle opening |
| (Threads) 「各位注意！」/「重磅消息！」 | Alarm tone — completely off-brand |

### 6.3 Forbidden Structural Patterns (Threads-Specific)

These are banned on Threads because they break the platform's conversational nature:

- ❌ LinkedIn-style structured formatting
- ❌ "Hook / TL;DR / Highlights / CTA" labels
- ❌ Bullet-point lists
- ❌ Numbered steps (convert to conversational flow instead)
- ❌ Headers or section breaks
- ❌ "Key takeaways:" summaries

## 8. Comment & Reply Tone

Social media doesn't end at the post. Replies and comments are brand touchpoints.

### 8.1 Reply Tone Rules

| Scenario | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Genuine question | Helpful, specific, generous | "Good question — the workflow uses Make.com's webhook module. I'll drop a link to the setup guide." |
| Positive feedback | Warm, grateful, brief | "Thanks! Glad this was useful 🙌" |
| Disagreement / criticism | Respectful, curious, non-defensive | "Interesting take — what's been your experience with it? Curious to hear more." |
| Spam / irrelevant | Ignore or hide | (No engagement) |
| Feature request / product Q | Redirect helpfully | "Great question — best to message us at [support channel] for the details. The team can help." |

### 8.2 Reply Rules

- Reply within 4 hours during business hours (HK timezone)
- Never argue — ask questions instead
- Never sell in replies — even if someone asks about the product, provide value first, link second
- Use the same platform-native tone as the post itself
- First-person replies — "I" not "we" on Threads, X. "We" acceptable on Facebook, LinkedIn.

## 9. Summary — NextMaven AI Social Media Voice in 10 Rules

1. Value first. Always. 95% of posts deliver value with zero selling. Earn trust through generosity.
2. Platform-native or don't post. What works on LinkedIn fails on Threads. Adapt everything — except the core insight.
3. Sound like a person, not a brand. Social media rewards authenticity. Write like you're sharing a discovery with a smart friend.
4. One post, one idea. Clarity beats comprehensiveness. Go deep on one thing.
5. Research before you write. Every post must reflect real conversations happening now — not assumptions from a content calendar written last month.
6. High info density, zero filler. Every sentence earns its place. If a line can be cut without losing meaning, cut it.
7. Design engagement, don't request it. Create content that naturally invites response — not "like if you agree."
8. Cantonese is Cantonese, English is English. No code-mixing. Each language has its own site and its own feel.
9. The Save Test is the ultimate test. If the audience wouldn't screenshot, bookmark, or send this to a colleague — it's not good enough.
10. Speed matters for news, quality matters for everything else. React fast to trends. Take your time with tutorials and frameworks.
